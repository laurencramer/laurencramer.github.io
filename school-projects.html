Project Courses - UCI

Senior Design Project (2019 - 20 weeks)

Course objective: Group supervised project in which students analyze, specify, design, construct, evaluate, and adapt a significant information processing system. Topics include team management, professional ethics, and systems analysis.

The project: We were tasked with creating a website for small theatre owners to simplify the process of acquiring rights to plays and shows, and providing a portal to access show resources.


Project in User Interface Software (2019 - 6 weeks)

Course objective: To complete an end-to-end user interface programming project based on an iterative design paradigm. Topics include requirements brainstorming, paper prototyping, iterative development, cognitive walk-through, quantitative evaluation, and acceptance testing.

The project: We created Cheqin, a mood-tracking mobile application that was designed to make mood-tracking easier and provide easily understandle data to increase the benefits of mood-tracking.


Project in Medical Informatics (2019 - 10 weeks)

Course objective: Students undertake significant quarter-long projects related to health informatics. Topics may include field evaluations of health care technologies, prototypes, iterative design, and system implementations.

The project: We designed a mobile application to assist those with mental illness who are seeking or attempting to seek professional health to improve communication of symptoms between patient and provider.


Project in Human-Computer Interaction Requirements and Evaluation (2018 - 10 weeks)

Course objective: Students undertake significant projects in the elicitation and specification of HCI requirements and the thorough evaluation of user interfaces.

The project: The purpose of this project was to provide an in-depth analysis and evaluation of an application, and we chose Sleep as Android. This project required extensive research done in verifying the usability of Sleep as Android along with its many features. It aims to provide validity to the proposed changes of the application through competitor and user research, user testing, and evaluation of potential redesigns for the key features identified through the research.


Project in Ubiquitous Computing (2018 - 10 weeks)

Course objective: Introduction to ubiquitous computing research methods, tools, and techniques. Prototyping, design, and evaluation of physical computing applications, smart environments, embedded systems, and future computing scenarios.

The project: This project consisted of designing, developing, and implementing our own Internet of Things technology. For the project, we identified a problem and designed a device with the intention of eliminating or reducing that problem. In this case, the problem consisted of empty coffee pots in a communal setting and the solution, a reminder-based, internet-connected coffee pot.


Project in Human-Computer Interaction (2017 - 10 weeks)

Course objective: Introduces students to user interface design techniques, design guidelines, and usability testing. Students gain the ability to design and evaluate user interfaces and become familiar with some of the outstanding research problems in HCI.

The project: This project consisted of redesigning an already existing website or application. My group chose Reddit, which we felt had a crude inferface compared to other popular websites.
